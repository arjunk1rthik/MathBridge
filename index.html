Why Learn From an Advanced High School Student?

As an advanced high school mathematics student, I understand what today's students experience because I recently learned—and mastered—the same concepts myself. Instead of relying on memorization or textbook definitions, I explain mathematics in simple, intuitive ways that make difficult ideas finally click. Because I'm closer in age to my students, lessons feel more like a conversation than a lecture. Many younger students feel more comfortable asking questions, admitting when they're confused, and working through mistakes without feeling intimidated. This supportive environment builds confidence, encourages curiosity, and helps students develop a genuine understanding of mathematics. I also know firsthand which concepts schools often move through too quickly. My lessons slow down where necessary, fill in those missing foundations, and connect ideas together so students understand not just how to solve problems, but why mathematics works. My goal is to help every student build confidence, strengthen critical thinking skills, and develop a love for problem solving that extends far beyond the classroom.
